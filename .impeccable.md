## Design Context

_This design context is inferred from the current repository because the user was unavailable for follow-up UX questions. It should be treated as the working default until the user refines it._

### Users
ReelForge appears to serve creators, marketers, indie builders, and technically comfortable makers who want to produce short-form videos quickly without relying on a traditional timeline editor.

Their working context is likely fast-paced and outcome-oriented: they are turning ideas, product clips, testimonials, memes, and promotional assets into social-ready videos, often iterating directly in the browser.

The core job to be done is:

- turn creative intent into short-form video outputs quickly
- reuse successful structures through templates
- move fluidly between code-like control and guided UI actions

### Brand Personality
The current product presentation suggests a brand that is:

- technical
- cinematic
- empowering

The emotional goals should be:

- confidence, so users feel in control of a powerful system
- momentum, so the product feels fast and creatively energizing
- clarity, so complexity never becomes intimidating
- a touch of delight, especially around gradients, motion, and template discovery

### Aesthetic Direction
The repository strongly suggests a dark-first visual system with vivid accent gradients, high contrast, rounded surfaces, and a hybrid “creative tool meets developer tool” tone.

Current implementation cues include:

- deep charcoal and near-black backgrounds
- indigo, blue, pink, amber, emerald, cyan, and fuchsia accents
- glassy overlays and soft border treatments
- rounded cards and pill-shaped controls
- bold gradients used to create energy and focus
- code-editor and creative-studio visuals living side by side

Working aesthetic direction:

- dark-first and high-contrast
- modern, polished, and cinematic
- expressive through accent color, but not noisy
- structured like a tool, not a marketing toy

Anti-direction inferred from the product:

- do not make it feel like a generic enterprise admin dashboard
- do not make it feel like a cluttered traditional NLE timeline editor
- do not make it feel childish, overly playful, or decoration-first

### Design Principles
1. **Dark-first clarity**
   Use dark surfaces, crisp contrast, and restrained elevation so complex creative workflows feel calm and legible.

2. **Precision with energy**
   Blend the confidence of a developer tool with the aspiration of a modern creative suite. Interfaces should feel exact, but never sterile.

3. **Outcome-oriented hierarchy**
   Keep primary actions obvious: save, publish, browse, use, export. The interface should always reinforce the user’s next useful step.

4. **Guided power over clutter**
   Favor progressive disclosure, grouped controls, and meaningful defaults instead of exposing every option at once.

5. **Templates as momentum**
   Treat templates as accelerators, not just content objects. Library, detail, and account flows should help users move from idea to editable result with minimal friction.
